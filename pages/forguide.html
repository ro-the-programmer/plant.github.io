Foraging guide

Foraging can be incredibly dangerous and harmful to the environment, but if done properly you can even help evironment and even get free food! This is why proper foraging is so important.

Here are a few general rules regarding foraging:

  1. Always be 100% certain of what you are a picking
  2. Only take bits of a plant, not the entirety
  3. Check if the plant is rare or protected
  4. Forage where it's allowed
With these rules most problems are avoided, and there are good reasons for these rules. Take rule 1, there are lots of plants, and thus also lots of plants that look alike. Some of these look a-likes can be deadly. Take the poison hemlock for example, it can kill you with just one leaf, but it looks nearly identical to wild parsley and carrot. Many unexperienced foragers have died due to this mistake, and thus you should always properly research the plant first, or ask a local expert. Rule 2 is to prevent overharvesting or picking important plants. There's a good reason that it's illegal in mnay countries to forage, this is to protect the wildlife. Becauce there have been many times that plants have gone extinct due to overharvesting, and not because of some big company, but because of individuals that didn't realise the harm they were causing.
